# Market Research

## Market Sizing: TAM / SAM / SOM

```
TAM (Total Addressable Market)
  → Everyone who could theoretically buy this
  → Used to show the size of the opportunity

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
  → The portion of TAM you can realistically reach
  → Based on geography, distribution, language, etc.

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
  → The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in 3–5 years
  → Based on your resources, competitive dynamics, go-to-market
```

**Two approaches:**

| Top-Down | Bottom-Up |
|----------|-----------|
| Start from industry reports (IDC, Gartner, etc.) | Start from unit economics |
| Divide down to your segment | Customers × price × frequency |
| Good for pitching | More defensible |

**Bottom-up example:**
```
Target: SME owners in Indonesia who need accounting software
→ ~4M SMEs in Indonesia
→ ~30% use digital tools (SAM: 1.2M)
→ We can reach ~5% in 3 years (SOM: 60,000)
→ ARPU: Rp 150,000/month = Rp 9B/month ARR potential
```

### Positioning Map

Pick two axes that matter most to your market (e.g., Price vs. Simplicity, Speed vs. Accuracy). Plot all competitors. Find the white space.

### Where to Research Competitors
- Their website, pricing page, case studies
- G2, Capterra, Trustpilot (read 1-star and 5-star reviews)
- LinkedIn (team size, growth signals, job postings)
- ProductHunt, App Store, Play Store
- Crunchbase (funding history)
- Their job postings (reveals what they're building next)

## Customer Discovery

### Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

People don't buy products. They hire them to get a job done.

```
Job Statement:
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]."

Example:
"When I'm reviewing team performance, I want to see progress on goals clearly,
so I can have productive 1:1s without spending an hour preparing."
```

Map every feature to a JTBD. If you can't, question whether to build it.

### Customer Segment Canvas

For each target segment:
```
Who are they? [demographics, role, company type]
What do they do today? [current workflow without your product]
What's the pain? [friction, cost, time, embarrassment]
What do they want to feel? [the emotional outcome]
What triggers them to look for a solution? [the moment of need]
Where do they discover solutions? [channels]
What would make them switch? [key buying criteria]
```

### Interview Synthesis

After 5–10 interviews, look for:
- **Patterns:** what pain keeps coming up?
- **Intensity:** how much does it hurt? Would they pay to fix it?
- **Existing solutions:** what are they doing now? (This is your real competition)
- **Language:** what words do they use? (Use these in your copy)

## Market Entry Framework

### Beachhead Market
Start with the narrowest possible segment where you can win completely, then expand. Better to dominate a niche than be mediocre in a broad market.

Criteria for a good beachhead:
- [ ] Has the pain urgently
- [ ] Has budget to solve it
- [ ] Is reachable (you can actually find and sell to them)
- [ ] Will benefit from and advocate for your product
- [ ] Leads to an adjacent larger market

### Land and Expand
```
Land → Expand
Win one team → Expand to department → Expand to company
Win one country → Expand regionally
Win one use case → Expand to adjacent use cases
```
