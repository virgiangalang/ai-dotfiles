# Business Strategy

## Situation Assessment First

Before any strategy work, answer:
1. What problem are we solving — for whom, and why now?
2. What does "winning" look like in 12 months?
3. What are we willing NOT to do?

## Frameworks

### SWOT Analysis
```
Strengths    → Internal advantages we can exploit
Weaknesses   → Internal gaps we must address or work around
Opportunities → External trends/gaps we can capture
Threats      → External forces that could kill us
```
Pair each S/W with an O/T for actionable insights, not just lists.

### Porter's Five Forces
- **Rivalry** — how intense is competition in this market?
- **New entrants** — how easy is it for others to enter?
- **Substitutes** — what else solves the customer's problem?
- **Buyer power** — how much leverage do customers have?
- **Supplier power** — how much leverage do vendors have?

Use to decide: is this market worth entering, and where is our defensibility?

### Blue Ocean (when red ocean is too crowded)
- **Eliminate** — what factors does the industry take for granted that we can remove?
- **Reduce** — what can we dial down below industry standard?
- **Raise** — what should we raise above industry standard?
- **Create** — what factors should we introduce that the industry has never offered?

### BCG Matrix (for portfolio decisions)
```
High Growth + High Share → Stars (invest)
High Growth + Low Share  → Question Marks (decide fast)
Low Growth + High Share  → Cash Cows (harvest)
Low Growth + Low Share   → Dogs (divest)
```

### Go-to-Market Strategy
1. **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — who exactly are we selling to first?
2. **Value proposition** — one sentence: "We help [ICP] do [job] so they can [outcome]"
3. **Channel** — how does the customer discover and buy? (direct, partner, PLG, content, paid)
4. **Pricing model** — freemium / subscription / usage / one-time / enterprise
5. **Launch sequence** — who are the first 10 customers, and how do we get them?

## Strategic Principles

**Focus over breadth:** A strategy that tries to serve everyone serves no one.

**Moats matter:** After product-market fit, build a moat. Options: network effects, switching costs, data advantage, brand, economies of scale.

**Speed of learning beats speed of execution:** In early stage, validate assumptions faster than you build features.

```
Company/Product: [name]
Mission: [why we exist]
3-Year Vision: [what we want to become]
12-Month Goal: [the one metric that matters]
Strategy: [how we'll get there — 3 key bets]
Key Risks: [top 3 things that could go wrong]
Not doing: [explicit list of what we're deprioritizing]
```

### Competitive Positioning Statement
```
For [target customer]
who [has this problem/need],
[product/company name] is a [market category]
that [key benefit / differentiator].
Unlike [primary competitor],
we [key advantage].
```
