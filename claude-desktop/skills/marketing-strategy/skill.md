# Marketing Strategy

## Skill Stack

- `market-research` (ECC) — ICP validation with evidence; TAM/SAM/SOM before committing to a channel
- `brand-strategy` — positioning and messaging must precede channel strategy; marketing without brand = noise
- `growth-metrics` — define CPL, MQL, CAC, ROAS targets before budget allocation
- `competitive-platform-analysis` (ECC) — understand competitor marketing strategy before designing yours
- `content-strategy` — content is the fuel for every organic channel; plan in parallel
- `data-analytics` — funnel metrics, cohort analysis, attribution data to guide channel decisions
- `marketing-campaign` (ECC) — end-to-end campaign execution after strategy is set

## Guardrails

- Never set a marketing budget before defining ICP — channels depend on where the ICP actually spends time
- Never recommend a channel mix without understanding current-state attribution data (what's working now?)
- Brand strategy (positioning, messaging) must exist before campaign creation — marketing without brand strategy produces inconsistent output
- Channel diversification is not a goal — focus beats spread in early-stage; diversify only when CAC is proven per channel
- Marketing goals must always translate to business outcomes (pipeline, revenue, ARR) — "impressions" is not an executive-level success metric
- The ICP is a hypothesis until validated with customer interviews and behavioral data — label it as such

## Mode 3: TOFU / MOFU / BOFU Mapping

| Funnel Stage | Goal | Metric | Content Type | Channel |
|-------------|------|--------|-------------|---------|
| **TOFU** (Awareness) | Generate demand | CPL, impressions, reach | Educational, problem-aware, entertaining | Social, SEO, YouTube, PR, TikTok |
| **MOFU** (Consideration) | Nurture interest | MQL rate, email CTR | Comparison, case studies, how-tos, webinars | Email, retargeting, LinkedIn |
| **BOFU** (Decision) | Drive conversion | SQL rate, demo booked, trial started | Demo, testimonials, pricing, free trial | Paid search, chat, direct sales |
| **Retention** | Reduce churn | NPS, upsell rate | Onboarding, tips, success stories | Email, in-app, community |
| **Advocacy** | Generate referrals | Referral rate, NPS | Reward programs, social proof | Referral program, community |

## Mode 4: Marketing Budget Framework

**Budget allocation by company stage (% of total marketing spend):**

| Stage | Awareness | Acquisition | Retention | Content/Brand | Tools/Ops |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Pre-PMF | 5% | 50% | 35% | 7% | 3% |
| Growth | 20% | 40% | 25% | 12% | 3% |
| Scale | 25% | 35% | 25% | 12% | 3% |
| Enterprise | 30% | 30% | 25% | 12% | 3% |

**Budget allocation by channel (growth stage):**
- Paid search: 25–35% (high intent, direct response, fastest to results)
- Paid social: 20–30% (awareness + retargeting + lookalikes)
- SEO/Content: 15–20% (compounding long-term, lowest CAC after 12 months)
- Email: 5–10% (lowest CAC, highest ROI on existing list — never underinvest here)
- Events/PR: 10–15% (brand building, enterprise relationships)
- Influencer/KOL: 5–10% (if ICP is on social platforms)

## Quality Gate

Before presenting a marketing strategy as complete:
- [ ] ICP defined with at least 3 behavioral/psychographic attributes (not just demographics)
- [ ] ICP validated with customer interviews or behavioral data — not pure assumption
- [ ] Channel mix justified based on ICP's actual media behavior (not just intuition)
- [ ] Marketing goals connected to business outcomes (pipeline, revenue, ARR) — not vanity metrics
- [ ] TOFU/MOFU/BOFU mapped with content type and channel for each stage
- [ ] Budget allocated with % breakdown and reasoning per channel
- [ ] Success metrics defined per channel (CPL, ROAS, organic traffic targets, MQL rate)
- [ ] 90-day execution roadmap with owners and timelines
- [ ] Negative ICP identified — who are you NOT marketing to?
